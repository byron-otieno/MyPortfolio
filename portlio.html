Digital Clock

A digital clock works by displaying the current time in a numeric format. It typically utilizes electronic components such as a quartz crystal oscillator to generate precise timing signals. These signals are then divided to produce regular pulses, which drive a series of counters that keep track of hours, minutes, and seconds. The resulting numerical values are converted into a format suitable for display on a digital screen, often using seven-segment LED or LCD displays. The clock is powered by electricity or batteries, and its timekeeping accuracy is maintained by the steady oscillation of the quartz crystal, ensuring that the displayed time is accurate and reliable.
